Breast Cancer

Breast cancer occurs when cells in the breast grow abnormally, often starting in milk ducts or lobules.

  • Symptoms: Lump, nipple changes, redness
  • Risk Factors: Age, family history, hormones
  • Detection: Mammogram, biopsy

Liver Cancer

Liver cancer begins in liver cells, often linked to hepatitis infections or cirrhosis from alcohol use.

  • Symptoms: Belly pain, weight loss, jaundice
  • Risk Factors: Hepatitis, alcohol, fatty liver
  • Detection: Blood test, ultrasound, CT scan

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is the uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells in the lungs, often caused by smoking.

  • Symptoms: Coughing blood, chest pain, shortness of breath
  • Risk Factors: Smoking, pollution, family history
  • Detection: Chest X-ray, CT, biopsy
