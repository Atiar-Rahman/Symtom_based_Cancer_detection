Understanding Lung Cancer

Learn about early signs, risk factors, prevention, and treatment options. Awareness is key to saving lives.

Early Signs and Symptoms

Lung cancer may not show noticeable symptoms in the early stages. Common signs include persistent cough, coughing up blood, chest pain, shortness of breath, unexplained weight loss, and fatigue. Early detection greatly improves treatment outcomes.

Risk Factors

Smoking is the leading cause of lung cancer, but non-smokers can also develop it due to secondhand smoke, air pollution, exposure to radon gas, asbestos, or genetic factors. Being aware of these risks can guide preventive actions.

Prevention Tips

Avoid smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke, maintain a healthy diet, exercise regularly, and test your home for radon. Lifestyle adjustments and regular medical checkups reduce the risk of developing lung cancer.

Treatment Options

Treatment depends on the type and stage of lung cancer. Options include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapy, and immunotherapy. Consult with a medical professional to create an appropriate treatment plan.

Quick Tips

  • Quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke.
  • Exercise regularly and eat a balanced diet.
  • Be aware of radon exposure in your home.
  • Schedule regular checkups and screenings.
  • Stay informed and spread awareness.
