Understanding Liver Cancer

Liver cancer is a serious disease, but early detection, awareness of risk factors, and healthy lifestyle choices can make a difference.

What is Liver Cancer?

Liver cancer occurs when abnormal cells in the liver grow uncontrollably, forming a tumor. It can start in the liver (primary liver cancer) or spread from other organs (secondary liver cancer). Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) is the most common type.

Early Signs and Symptoms

Early liver cancer often has no symptoms. When they appear, common signs include unexplained weight loss, loss of appetite, upper abdominal pain, jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes), fatigue, and nausea. Regular check-ups are crucial for early detection.

Risk Factors

Several factors increase the risk of liver cancer, including chronic hepatitis B or C infection, cirrhosis, heavy alcohol use, obesity, type 2 diabetes, and exposure to aflatoxins (toxins from moldy foods). Understanding your risk factors helps in prevention and monitoring.

Prevention and Healthy Lifestyle

Preventive measures include getting vaccinated for hepatitis B, avoiding excessive alcohol, maintaining a healthy weight, eating a balanced diet, regular exercise, and limiting exposure to harmful chemicals. Early detection through liver function tests and imaging can save lives.

Treatment Options

Treatment depends on the stage and type of liver cancer. Options include surgical removal, liver transplant, ablation therapy, embolization, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, and chemotherapy. Consulting a medical professional is essential to determine the best plan.

Living with Liver Cancer

Support from healthcare providers, family, and community is important. Emotional health, diet management, and monitoring treatment side effects play a key role in maintaining quality of life during and after treatment.

Quick Tips

  • Get vaccinated for hepatitis B.
  • Limit alcohol and avoid smoking.
  • Maintain a healthy diet and exercise regularly.
  • Screen regularly if at high risk.
  • Consult a doctor if symptoms appear.
